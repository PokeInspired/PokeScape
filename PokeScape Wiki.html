PokeScape 0.3 Wiki

A collection side game where Old School RuneScape activities feed a RuneScape themed monster-card album.

Work in progress: PokeScape 0.3 is an early build. Some content, cards, evolutions, Shiney cards, Wilderness cards, rewards, and balancing may not be available yet.

What PokeScape Is

PokeScape is a collection game that runs alongside Old School RuneScape. Normal OSRS actions can earn PokeScape shards, runes, essences, packs, cards, map rewards, and evolution materials.

The main loop is simple: play OSRS, earn resources, open animated packs, reveal cards on the game screen, fill the album, trade duplicates, evolve cards, and chase rarer rewards through the wheel, bosses, clues, and treasure maps.

New players start with an empty collection: no cards, shards, packs, runes, essences, stones, fragments, Shard Dust, or free spins.

Current Main Features

Packs
Silver, Mithril, Rune, and Shiney packs open on the main game screen. The pack appears first, then plays the opening animation, then deals revealable cards.
Album
View collected cards, zoom into card images, navigate with arrows, filter, sort, and preview future locked slots.
Wheel
Spend shards or free spins to roll cards, shards, packs, loot bags, free spins, and Wilderness Fragments. The wheel spins to the already rolled reward, then plays the reward animation.
Duplicate Trade
Trade duplicate cards for shards or Shard Dust using the full-screen duplicate exchange and reward animation.
Evolutions
Some cards evolve using runes, essences, and evolution stones.
Drops
Runes and essences can come from XP drops, monsters, bosses, and clue reward caskets.
Treasure Maps
Buy Wildy or World maps, follow directional clues, click the treasure casket, watch the prize reel, then claim the rolled treasure.

Card Rarities

Rarity colours are used during pack and wheel card reveals.

Common: white Uncommon: green Rare: blue Epic: purple Utility: yellow

Trainer and energy cards are treated as utility cards visually, and they roll in the same space as uncommon cards for packs and wheel card rewards.

Normal packs do not include third evolutions, legendary cards, Eevee evolutions, Mew, Mewtwo, or the three legendary birds. Those are intended for other systems such as evolution or future rewards. Shiney rolls also exclude Shiney legendary cards.

If you own a pack, you can open it without spending shards. If you choose to buy a pack with shards, you can cancel before opening and get the shards back.

Wheel

The PokeScape Wheel is opened from the Collection tab. The wheel is adaptive to different RuneLite canvas sizes.

  • Minimum spin is 10 shards.
  • Free spins cost 0 shards.
  • Free spins always use the same odds as a 10 shard spin.
  • Higher shard stakes improve some reward odds.
  • Wilderness fragments start at 0.1% on a 10 shard spin.
  • Wilderness fragments max at 5% at 500 shards.

Shards

Shards are the main currency for packs and wheel spins.

  • XP shard gain is fixed at 1000 XP per shard.
  • If a player needs 200 XP and gains 600 XP, they receive 1 shard and the tracker rolls over to 600 XP remaining.
  • XP shard gain can be disabled in the PokeScape settings.
  • Duplicate card trading can also give shards.
  • The wheel can also award shards.

Shard Dust

Shard Dust is the Shiney currency. It is used to buy Shiney Packs.

  • Duplicate Shiney cards trade for 10 Shard Dust each.
  • Normal duplicate trades have a 20% chance to convert their shard payout into Shard Dust instead.
  • When normal duplicate trades convert, the dust amount is ceil(shards x 1.5).

The duplicate exchange opens as a full-screen overlay. It now scales to different canvas sizes.

Left-click adds a duplicate to the trade. Right-click removes one. Normal card trades have a 20% chance to award Shard Dust instead of shards; when that happens, the trade animation uses the Shard Dust reward image.

Evolution

Some cards cannot be collected directly from packs. They must be evolved from earlier cards.

  • Evolution consumes 1 copy of the original card.
  • Evolution grants 1 copy of the evolved card.
  • If the evolved card has a Shiney version, evolution has a 4% chance to also award the Shiney version.
  • Costs can include runes, essences, and evolution stones.
  • If you do not have the required items, clicking the requirement plays a rejected sound.

Boss Pack Rewards

Credited boss kills can roll a pack at 1:5. Easier bosses tend to award Silver or Mithril packs, while harder bosses and raids tend to award Rune packs.

When a boss pack is awarded, it can upgrade into a Shiney Pack. Easy bosses upgrade at 1:125, medium bosses at 1:100, hard bosses at 1:75, and raid bosses at 1:50. Shiney Pack rewards do not roll Shiney legendary cards.

Examples include Obor, Bryophyta, Scurrius, Giant Mole, Barrows brothers, King Black Dragon, Kalphite Queen, Dagannoth Kings, Kraken, Zulrah, Vorkath, Phantom Muspah, Abyssal Sire, Cerberus, Alchemical Hydra, God Wars bosses, Nex, TzTok-Jad, TzKal-Zuk, The Doom of Mokhaiotl, and raid bosses.

Buried Treasure Maps

Treasure Maps live on the Maps tab. Buy a map first, then click Begin when you are ready to start tracking it. Only one map can be active at a time. If you die while a map is active, the active map is lost and no reward is given.

  • Wildy Maps cost 300 shards and must be tracked inside the Wilderness.
  • World Maps cost 150 shards and lead to non-Wilderness locations around the rest of the game world.
  • The Track button gives direction-only chat clues such as north, south, east, or west. It does not reveal the exact tile count.
  • When you stand on the correct tile and press Track, a treasure casket appears on the main game screen.
  • The casket does not open instantly. Click the closed casket to open it, then the prize reel rolls and freezes on the actual reward.
  • The reward is not added until the reveal is complete and you click to continue.

Player Notes

  • PokeScape is designed as a side collection game, so rewards are plugin-only and do not create OSRS items.
  • Many systems are still being balanced. Costs, rates, reward tables, and available cards may change between builds.
  • Wilderness Cards are still being developed, with reveal and fragment systems being tested and balanced.
  • Some cards are intentionally kept out of normal packs so they can be earned through evolution or future systems.
